[EJEMPLO REAL APROBADO]
Imaginate lo que es liquidar tus cuentas en 5 segundos sin pasar por una mesa OTC. Suena como un sueño para cualquier tesorería corporativa en Argentina. La empresa Chuky acaba de habilitar este off-ramp directo a cuentas bancarias por CBU/CVU.

El ecosistema local de cobros y pagos ya está hiper acostumbrado a la automatización inmediata. Pero la liquidación siempre tarde de 1 a 3 días. En los dias volatiles (que en argentina son mas de lo que a cualquier humano le gustaria), el tiempo de settlement es lo más importante. Especialmente para los CFOs.

Sin embargo, esta velocidad de Chuky se explica por su precio: 3.5% de comisión. Para mover volumen diario de forma constante, este costo transaccional vuelve inviable la operación de cualquier VASP o PyME.

Asique acá va a tener que primar la decisión de los managers. La liquidez inmediata es útil para resolver emergencias o cuellos de botella temporales, pero no creo que vaya a reemplazar una arquitectura de ruteo financiero planificada.

Hoy por hoy ¿Qué vale más velocidad o costos bajos? Habrá alguna alternativa que tenga ambos?

[EJEMPLO REAL APROBADO #2: ENSAYO EN INGLÉS - CRÍTICA A BANCA TRADICIONAL]
Just got an email from BBVA, and I almost broke the keyboard.

In 58 days, the monthly maintenance fee for my account will be ARS 55,000 (~$36 USD) unless I spend more than ARS 1,000,000 per month (~$666 USD) on their products.

Fifty-five thousand pesos for an account I barely use. Honestly, I can’t wait to close it.

What’s frustrating is that traditional banks still benefit from being deeply embedded in systems many people can’t easily avoid.

If you earn income from abroad, need local credit, or must navigate Argentina’s endless compliance requirements, you’ll likely maintain relationships with institutions you’d never voluntarily choose today.

Meanwhile, fintechs and stablecoin-powered financial products have built faster, cheaper, and often dramatically better experiences.

Need international transfers? Stablecoin wallet.  
Need USD/EUR exposure? Belo, Wallbit.  
Need spending cards? ARQ.  
Need yield on idle cash? Fiwind, Ripio.  
Need a good payment app? All the above.

The one thing traditional banks still have a meaningful moat around is large credit lines. That’s it: credit underwriting.

The day fintechs and on-chain financial products can consistently offer credit cards with limits above ARS 5M ($3,000 USD) at competitive rates will be the day we witness one of the largest migrations of users in financial history.

People aren’t staying because they love their banks. They’re staying because they are trapped.

Every time I receive one of these “we’re increasing our fees again” emails, I’m reminded how overdue that disruption is.

Is this kind of banking complacency common everywhere, or is Argentina just operating on hard mode?
